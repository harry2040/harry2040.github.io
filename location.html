GROVELAND California

Groveland, CA: Your Gateway to Yosemite's Gold Rush History and Outdoor Adventures

Groveland is a charming, historic town nestled in the Sierra Nevada foothills, serving as a popular gateway to Yosemite National Park. This former Gold Rush town offers visitors a unique blend of:

  • Rich History: Step back in time at the Iron Door Saloon, reputed to be California's oldest continuously operating saloon, and explore the Groveland Yosemite Gateway Museum to learn about the area's Gold Rush past and the construction of the Hetch Hetchy water project.
  • Proximity to Yosemite: Located on Highway 120, Groveland provides convenient access to Yosemite National Park, making it an ideal base for exploring the park's iconic landmarks and natural wonders.
  • Outdoor Recreation: Enjoy a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking to scenic waterfalls like Carlon Falls, whitewater rafting on the Tuolumne River, and golfing at Pine Mountain Lake. The surrounding Stanislaus National Forest also offers ample opportunities for exploration.
  • Charming Atmosphere: Groveland boasts a quaint downtown area with local shops, restaurants, and lodging options, providing a welcoming and relaxed atmosphere for visitors.

Whether you're seeking to delve into California's Gold Rush history, embark on a Yosemite adventure, or simply enjoy the beauty of the Sierra Nevada, Groveland offers a memorable and authentic experience.
